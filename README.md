# 💳 Wevolv Payments Strategy (QA x Data Project)
## 📌 Overview

This project showcases my experience working alongside a QA Engineer during my internship at a high-growth SaaS startup. The focus was on evaluating and stress-testing payment infrastructure decisions for a subscription-based mobile platform.

The project demonstrates my ability to:

* Translate business requirements into technical evaluation criteria
* Work cross-functionally with QA and engineering teams
* Analyse system-level trade-offs (cost, scalability, compliance)
* Think critically about backend architecture in SaaS environments

---

## 🎯 Project Objective

To evaluate and compare payment processing solutions suitable for a subscription-first SaaS platform, with emphasis on:

* Scalability across international markets
* Subscription billing capabilities
* Cost efficiency at scale
* Reliability and compliance

---

## 🧠 Key Concepts Explored

### 1. Subscription Billing Architecture

* Recurring billing logic (monthly/yearly subscriptions)
* Proration handling during upgrades/downgrades
* Coupon and discount systems
* Churn and retention tracking

### 2. Payment Gateway Evaluation Criteria

* Transaction fees and cost structures
* Multi-currency and localisation support
* Developer experience (APIs, SDKs, documentation)
* Security standards (PCI compliance, fraud detection)
* System reliability and uptime

### 3. QA Perspective (My Contribution)

Working alongside a QA Engineer, I contributed to:

* Identifying edge cases in billing flows (failed payments, retries, renewals)
* Evaluating test scenarios for subscription lifecycle events
* Supporting validation of payment workflows from a user-experience perspective
* Highlighting potential failure points in international transactions

---

## ⚙️ Comparative Analysis

### Stripe (Primary Solution)

**Strengths:**

* Strong subscription billing infrastructure
* High-quality developer tooling (APIs & SDKs)
* Built-in analytics (MRR, churn, retention)
* Global payment support
* Robust fraud prevention systems

**Limitations:**

* Higher transaction fees at scale
* Limited pricing flexibility for smaller companies

---

### Alternative Gateways (Exploratory)

**Adyen:**

* Strong in European markets
* Lower interchange fees via local acquiring
* Better suited for large-scale enterprise operations

**Braintree:**

* Supports digital wallets (e.g., PayPal)
* Flexible payment mix
* Slightly less intuitive developer experience

---

## 📊 Key Insights

* Early-stage SaaS platforms benefit from fast integration over cost optimisation
* Subscription-native tooling is critical for reducing engineering overhead
* Payment localisation becomes essential during international expansion
* A single payment provider introduces long-term risk (vendor lock-in)

---

## 🚀 Strategic Outcome

### Short-Term Recommendation

* Adopt a single, developer-friendly payment provider to accelerate product development

### Long-Term Considerations

* Introduce multi-gateway architecture
* Explore local payment processors for regional cost optimisation
* Implement billing orchestration for flexibility
